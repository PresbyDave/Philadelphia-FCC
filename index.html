Welcome

We are a Preaching Station of the U.S. Presbytery of the Free Church of Scotland (Continuing).

What Is a Preaching Station?

A preaching station is a recognized gathering for regular public worship that has not yet attained the status of an established congregation. It remains under the oversight of the Presbytery, which appoints a Session and Moderator to provide pastoral care, exercise church discipline, and ensure the regular preaching of the gospel and administration of the sacraments.

Who We Are

We are a confessional Presbyterian church that holds to historic Reformed theology as summarized in the Westminster Standards.

Our desire is to glorify God through faithful preaching, reverent worship, evangelism, and the discipleship of God's people.

Whether you are new to the Christian faith, seeking a church home, or visiting the area, we invite you to join us for worship.

What We Believe

We hold as the confession of our faith the original 1647 version of the Westminster Confession of Faith, along with the Larger and Shorter Catechisms as our subordinate standards, believing them to be faithful summaries of the doctrines taught in Holy Scripture.

The Holy Scriptures

We believe the Holy Scriptures of the Old and New Testaments are the inspired, infallible Word of God, the only supreme rule of faith and life, and fully sufficient to make known all that is necessary for God’s glory and man’s salvation.

The Triunity of God

We believe there is one living and true God, eternally existing in three Persons: the Father, the Son, and the Holy Ghost, equal in power and glory.

Jesus Christ

We believe Jesus Christ is the eternal Son of God, fully God and fully man, the only Mediator between God and man, who lived, died, rose again, and reigns forever as Lord and Saviour.

Salvation

We believe salvation is by God’s grace alone, through faith alone in Jesus Christ alone, according to His eternal purpose, and received apart from the works of the law.

The Church

We believe Jesus Christ gathers, preserves, and governs His Church through the ministry of the Word, the sacraments, and biblical discipline, for the glory of God and the salvation of His people.

The Sacraments

We believe Christ instituted two sacraments, Baptism and the Lord’s Supper, as holy ordinances to signify and seal His covenant of grace and to strengthen the faith of believers.

Public Worship

We believe God is to be worshiped only in the ways He has appointed in His Word, with reverence, simplicity, and according to the Regulative Principle of Worship.

The Lord's Day

We believe the Lord’s Day is the Christian Sabbath, to be kept holy unto God through public and private worship, with ordinary work and recreations laid aside except for works of necessity and mercy.

Our Worship

Our worship seeks to follow the pattern given in Scripture.

Each Lord's Day gathering includes:

  • The reading of God's Word
  • The experiential preaching of God's Word
  • Prayer
  • Congregational singing of the Psalms without instrumental accompaniment
  • *The administration of the sacraments: Baptism and the Lord's Supper*

We seek simplicity, reverence, and faithfulness in all elements of worship.

*Baptism is administered as needed to new converts and to the children of believers. The Lord's Supper is observed quarterly during designated Communion seasons.*

About the Free Church of Scotland (Continuing)

The Free Church of Scotland (Continuing) was reconstituted in 2000 following a constitutional division within the Free Church of Scotland. It maintains that it is the lawful continuation of the historic Free Church, remaining faithful to the principles of the 1843 Protest. The name “Continuing” was adopted only to distinguish it administratively from the remaining body. Central to its identity is the historic right of continued protest against unconstitutional actions by church courts without being subject to discipline.
